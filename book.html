The text of the book

The two united in the past, a place to build, and spells to cast.
Their power grew, and took the land a people round, as they had planned.
A key without a lock they made of gold and gems, and overlaid
with spells, atool for men to wield to force the powers of Good to yield.
But armies came, their weapons bared, while evil was unprepared.
The Hart was followed by the Crowns and Moon, and people of the towns.
The tWo were split; one got away but she, when came the judgement day,
did break the key, and sent the rocks to boxes four, with magic locks.
In doing so, She fell behind as He escaped. She was confined
among here own; Her very lair became her prison and despair.
The place was ruined, torn apart a left with chains around the heart
of evil power - but the key was never found in the debris.
He knows not where She dwells today. He set the minions path, the way
to lift His Temple high again with tools of flesh with mortal men.
Many have gone to die in water, flame, in earth or sky.
They did not bear the key of old that must be found - the orb of gold.
Beware, my friend, for you shall fall unless you have the wherewithall
To find and search the boxes four and then escape forevermore.
But with the key, you might succeed in throwing down His power and greed.
Destroy the key when you are done and then rejoice, the battle won.

A GM's note to the players: Please note this poem is less relevant to the campaign now then when we started. I intended it to be more relevant, but it turns out we veered off course. The important things are the four elemental gems, the two people and the golden orb.

The companions of Green Crag left to visit Venezia in hopes of finding someone with more information about the powe and the book in general. Their only lead is in the form of an introductory letter to Leodrin of the Gods.

The three companions, in Venezia, learned very little concerning the poem, their mission and even the world. What little they did learn was there were two humans by the names of Lowan and Neptene. The two conspired to usurp the gods powers. It seems they developed tools that at the very least frightened the gods. Denstra Kairna suggested seeking his Mentotr in the ruins of Neth. The companions head deep into the Salt Marshes by boat, and then by foot to the foothills of the Bolas peaks.

Caden lost his life in the foothills of Bolas from wolves, and was given to a Giant Eagle for a burial.

Aldham and Trust continued to Neth, and met up with a friend of Caden's.

They meet the Master of Neth, and tells them to find the gems and orbs, Crawl on the floor of the sea. Walk among the clouds. Dance among the blind. Tiptoe among the chained. And find the lost battlefield. He also offered them great power if they return them to him.

After reading many of the Master’s books, they left and returned to where they stored Ultai’s Swift Net, but a Troll was nearby. The gnome was killed before the giant eagle returned, and brought them to safety.

Aldham and Trust were brought into the Bolas Peaks to the Great Eagle’s Eyrie.

The Giant Eagles brought them across the plains and near the Drowned City, and gave them the warning that a dragon lives in the swamp.

As they got close to the ruins of Zostus, the met a Dwarven historan by the name of Travon, and headed into a fairly intact portion of Zostus.

Underneath the swamp, much of Zostus’ temple district remained intact. Through working with a group of kobolds they killed worknig with a group of kobolds they killed Ssthixis, and Rova the High king of the kobolds gave the party Rusty, a miniature iron golem with the ability to transform into a submersible vehicle.

Rusty mention his friend Onda, a sentient trident, and that Onda would likely help in their mission.

With Onda in hand and Rusty at their sides, they left Zostus without disturbing the ancient black dragon, Xerneas.

After arriving back in Venezia, the companions are made aware of the kuo-toan problem plaguing the coast. They agree to help Queen Woanna in exchange for a Caravel.

When waiting for Aldham to return from Green Crag, Varis, Travok and Trust seek Enzo’s aid, but end up alerting the Slaver’s that Enzo and the Thieves’ Guild are moving against them.

Through some amount of trickery and luck, the companions were able to get to the sea hag coven’s lair without having to fight through the masses of the kuo-toa, and are able to defeat the hags, and recover Ultai’s gem. With the death of the sea hags, the kuo-toans cease their attacks on the coast. The queen gives the companions a caravel as agreed upon.

With Ultai’s Unyielding Reach in their control, the companions set out to seek more information concerning the elemental gems.

In the library of XXXXX, Trust discovered tales of an ancient kingdom spanning more land than any island. Also in Eclavue, the companions received a veiled threat signed by ML.

After returning to Venezia, the companions learned of the actions of the Slaver’s against Enzo. Jerome approached them to find out the murderer of Enzo. By doing this they endangered their families and loved ones.

During a visit to the Dwarven library in Gell, Trust and Varis discovered an ancient map before the shattering of the lands in the wake of Lowan’s war with the gods.
